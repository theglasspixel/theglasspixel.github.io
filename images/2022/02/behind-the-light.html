Behind the Light - digital abstract art

Please imagine an explosion on a ship. Well, not really. Wait a second... I know: imagine a flickering of light hitting the corner of your eye just as you walk out of your apartment building on a Sunday morning. One of those mornings drenched in sunshine and... Well, also not really. Okay, imagine an artist in a dark, stuffy room, his trusty computer humming away in the corner, pulsating slowly as if waiting, waiting, waiting, as computers do. Then it says, "your image is ready." The pulsating accelerates a little. "Does it satisfy your aesthetic standards? Will it generate revenue? Will it please the algorithms? Can it conceivably be considered acceptable abstract art?"
