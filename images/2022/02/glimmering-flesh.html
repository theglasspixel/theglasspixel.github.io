Glimmering Flesh - digital abstract art

It was late summer in the South Towers when we met at Frank's apartment.

He showed me his collection of collages, which were actually a bunch of small ceramic bottles full to the brim with colorful liquids.

Smell them, he said. They're all a bit hallucinogenic, but won't kill you.

I declined the suggestion and turned my eyes to the painting you see above this text.

It's my girlfriend, he said.

Really? I replied. A bit pornographic, don't you think?

It's abstract art of the highest order! He shouted, finger pointing at my nose for some reason. A visual masterpiece!

I said no more and moved on to the row of herbivorous plants growing by the window and calmly chatting among themselves about the pros and cons of mutagenic rain and its effects on man-made large structures.

They're all going to die, I heard one of the plants say.

Then they all looked at me and kept quiet.

I never got to meet frank's girlfriend, but after his disappearance, I managed to sneak this painting out of his place.

So, here it is.
