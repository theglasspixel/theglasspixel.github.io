Abstract 9 - digital abstract art

The bars close at three a.m. on Saturday nights, so I got home about six in the morning, after eating breakfast in a crummy little bistro down in the lower floors.

A giant fern plant, kept looking at me with a desperate look so I noped out of there without even paying the bill, which was for the better since I had no money anyway.

I went straight to bed, but the buzzer rang. It's one of those buzzers that will send you into a days' long hallucination if you don't attend to it so I went over and opened the door.

Three people came into the room. They sat down quietly and without looking at me, and then one of them showed me a sort of fluorescent card.

It said, "Google Employee nº 01010010101001010."

"Are we understood then?" One of them said while staring at the image you now see above this text.

I nodded and opened the door to let them out.

Now, I have no idea what any of this means, but I'm leaving here this little piece of abstract digital art, anyway.

Bless the algorithms. Bless them all.
