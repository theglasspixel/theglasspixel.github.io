Frank's Wild Paintings

well, Frank settled down in the city, his wild years long gone and forgotten, and every day, with a limp and a cigarette, he walks down from his two-room apartment to the library downtown where he spends the days reading and sleeping and forgetting about the images in his head.

and every day he floats through the tall windows and opens his wings of golden shimmer against the rain-drenched smog while glass facades melt under red lights.

from above he looks at all the junkies and prostitutes and men with ties and gray suits and balding heads that climb from window to window up the slippery buildings, up from the rivers of light and filth that stick to the ground in straight, unmovable lines.

helicopters wiz by in whirling, menacing dances, like dragonflies seeking for copulating partners.

his golden wings cover the city as pulsating sirens draw red lines through the smog, and sky-scrapers blossom to the darkness above like rows of gray tulips sinking their roots beneath the city.

Frank opens his eyes to the blurry library: it’s time for another cigarette.
