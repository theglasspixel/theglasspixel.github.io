The Unbearable Weight of Modesty

People will often approach me with a shy smile and downcast gaze, and question me about my artistic career. "How is it," they ask, "that you can be so successful, so brilliant, so famous and beloved, while we, the rest of humanity, remain in this uneducated darkness. How can we ever hope to achieve, or to even barely touch your greatness?"

This sort of thing amuses me, obviously.

But they insist: "and your writing prowess! Step away Joyce! Hide in shame Shakespeare!"

Well, I can't help but to rush back home, sit in front of my trusty new computer and quickly mix a few colors, maybe a little crimson, then a touch of absinthe green... In an instant, a new masterpiece for the ages is born.

"Ah!" I then say to myself, "but don't forget the keywords, those tasty morsels of nothingness that feed our lords, the algorithms!"

Oh, if only the common man could even begin to understand how my genius, my inner fire, my unbridled modesty, my soaring status as an abstract digital artist, is oh-so chained to the constraints of the modern world!

The old conjurations are useless! New keywords are needed, a new seo for awakening the angels and demons of printed abstract art (*canvas, fabric, wood: it's your choice! just click the little buttons on the side!*), wall art, and all things art and artistic and digital and so on.

Ahhhh! I already feel the bitterness and cynicism receding into the silent void! Fame and fortune await just around the corner...
