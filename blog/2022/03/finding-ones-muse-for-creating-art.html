Finding One’s Muse for Creating Art

Starting at the young age of two, when my parents gave me a hook and fishing line, I spent most of my time down by the river, watching as nature reclaimed all that man as spent millennia building.

One summer morning I caught a big old tree with a bait my grandad had taught me: a small mirror hidden inside a silk-line fastened acorn. The mirror was painted red. Blood-red. That's how I started to paint, actually, by dabbing paint all over my reflected image.

Anyway, having caught this giant tree, at first, I struggled to pull it out of the water ---I was only two, after all---, but after a few minutes, the thing finally stopped resisting and stepped onto dry land, towering over me.

"You should paint," it said, "at home, alone, instead of going about this fishing business, bothering everybody."

"I like it here in the sun," I said. "And I like the green sky. it's pretty. Prettier than my bedroom's ceiling."

"Do you know why the sky is green?" The big old cedar retorted. "It's because we are taking it all back. the houses, the bridges, the skyscrapers, every inch of land you people burned, polluted, destroyed. Do you understand why?"

I shrugged and ate a roasted acorn.

"I'm only two," I said. "You shouldn't expect me to know things."

"Do you know anything at all?" the tree scoffed. "No man anywhere knows anything worth knowing, that's what I think."

For the rest of that morning, we sat down by the river and kept silent, watching the clouds of pollen and seeds and dust whirling above us, at times even obscuring the cloud-reaching towers downstream.

"Do you have a muse already?" It suddenly said.

"A muse?"

"Yes, something to inspire you. Something that will make your art as fruitful as nature itself." It pointed to the sky. "Look at those green clouds up there, filled with life-giving water. You should try to be like that. But you'll need a muse for that. No man can do it alone."

"I'm only two, mister... Besides, aren't you all trying to kill humanity? Why would you care about my art?"

"We're building a museum of man. It will feature two atom bombs, a barrel of crude oil, and lots of works of art."

At lunchtime, I walked back home with the tree closely following me, and we sat down in the kitchen eating the meal my mom had prepared.
