Bill's Art Exhibition

The last time I went to an art exhibition was maybe a year ago when my friend Bill was courteously invited by the Metro Tower Art Complex's administration to showcase a few of his most popular sound-and-vision happenings.

So, I had to go all the way over to the top floors of one of the central towers, down by the seashore. It took me almost half a day just to get there, and then it turned out that the entrance was down on the ground level. Yes, on level zero, deep in forest and swamp territory.

Fortunately, the organizers were gracious enough to provide everybody with masks, boots and all the rest... And by everybody, I mean me, myself, some other guy, and two mean-looking women heavily tattooed with political logos of the anarchic-biosynthesis persuasion.

One of those nice ladies had a vine plant growing out of her backside and wrapping around her torso and neck. They both smelled of chlorophyll and fermentation. Very nasty indeed.

Anyway, as I later discovered, the trip down to the ground was in itself the first 'happening': one of Bill's famous transavantgarde/environmental/neo-cyber-genetic (a mouthful, I know!) performance pieces that used to be so snubbed by the art intelligentsia but then became all the rage. Nowadays, as we all know very well, that kind of stuff is completely passé, even ridiculed.

For spacial guidance, we had a single bright-green butterfly-like thing that flopped around in the air, completely disappearing for long periods of time during which we just sat around watching the fist-sized mosquitos trying to pierce our protective garments.

Obviously, I can't describe the art pieces proper, due to the increasingly ridiculous non-disclosure agreements that now plague the art world... I did try once to get an art-critic license from the central office of intellectual property, but my request was summarily refused, as they now don't accept anyone older than sixteen years old or without viral-ready limbic-chips embedded since the age of two.

"Only viral art is proper art!" and all that...

Anyway, why am I even writing about things I can't write about? I guess I can say that I very much enjoyed the whole thing, and would recommend the experience to anyone who can muster the courage to venture down there.

Stay tuned for more posts, my friends!

Oh, and if any of you know about Bill's whereabouts, please contact me, or the authorities, or anyone really. I miss getting drunk and high with ol'Bill...
