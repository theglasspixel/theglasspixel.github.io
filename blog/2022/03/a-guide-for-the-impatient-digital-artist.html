A Guide for the Impatient Digital Artist

My troubles began when I started hanging out with a spider... I know, I know! you don't really need to lecture me about the dangers of socializing too often with arachnidas, arthopods, or any other exoskeleton-covered animals.

Anyway, there I was, busy messing about with the Colores Aglaia thing, cursing at myself for never being able to get the damned image right the way I wanted it, my trusty new computer huffing and puffing in a cloud of scarlet-tinted vapor (boy, do these new generation machines have an awful breath!), when it suddenly hit me: the colors were all wrong! I mean, you know that feeling, right? when all the colors suddenly start reeking like the corpse of an ancient forest and you feel like you're sitting on a chair made of pine-cones?

Anyway, that's when the spider on my shoulder whistled loudly and said "je vous en prie! that pixel isn't right, I tell you! What kind of artist are you?"

"Thank you for calling me an artist," I said with my usual witty humor.

"Hardly!" the spider (I call him Frank, but you can call him whatever) said. "That pixel there needs to be a shade of amber!"

"No one will care, Frank," I said. "It's just a fucking pixel."

"Sacre bleu!" Frank jumped up and down, shaking his tiny legs, shooting slim silky strands all over the place. "And on top of that you're also a terrible writer! Where's all the keywords? Think of the search engines you dumb 'artist'! Think of the search engines!"

"I am! otherwise I wouldn't be doing this."

Anyway, I could swear this whole thing had a point, but for the life of me I can't remember what it was. "Frank, do you know---"

"It's always best to begin an artwork by conceptualizing the psychological experience, the mental object you want to share with your fellow human beings, and---"

"What the fuck are you talking about, Frank?"

"If you don't fucking want to do it, then I---" Frank poured two fingers of scotch on his glass with a loud sigh and eight downcast and very sad eyes. "Look," he regained some energy, "as a digital artist, you---"

"Thank you for calling me an artist." (a joke is always funnier if you keep repeating it!)

"---I, hmmm... the problematic of the modern digital artist in a web-connected world dominated by algorithms made by socially-inept geeks working for giant ads-corporations intent on turning us all into shopping addict fascists---"

"Frank---"

"Qu'est-ce que c'est?"

"Your blood pressure, Frank. Just calm down, will you?"

"You should be a gardener! That's it. You mow the grass and I sit in a tree, eating some delicious flies," Frank said, taking a gulp of scotch. "What do you think of that?"

"I think the computer farted again. We better get out of here."
