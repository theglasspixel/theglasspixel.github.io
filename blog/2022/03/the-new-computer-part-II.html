The New Computer :: part II

My new computer is growing up nicely. It now occupies an entire room in my apartment, and is fully integrated with the water plumbing. It even has a little system to collect rain water from the veranda which it then transmogrifies into these little drops of greenish liquid that emits the strangest, dreamiest sounds imaginable.

Adding to that, I have now installed a brand new imaging software. It arrived the day before last in two vials, carefully enclosed inside a glass box, so as not to poison the air, killing everyone from here down to the fiftieth floor of the tower.

The software uses the latest developments in limbic-to-limbic engagement, and, of course, requires an online account with a lengthy 'privacy-enhancement' disclosure to which I had to 'agree' in order to use this thing. This means I now receive personalized advertisement in both my night-dreams and my day-dreams...

Anyway, the good thing about this new version is that I can sit down and do nothing---absolutely nothing!--- while my trusty computer scans my brain (privacy-enhancements apply!) and itself dreams-up of new images according to a few settings I have decided beforehand.

So now my artistic work is simply to cycle through all the thousands of images my computer generates, looking for something I might find worthwhile.

Up until now---but it's only been one day!--- I haven't found a single one, but I'm very hopeful that very soon a masterpiece will emerge.

What do you think? Am I going to get lucky?

Well, for now I'll just sit back and continue to do nothing, just like all true artists do. (Or so I'm told by my computer...)
