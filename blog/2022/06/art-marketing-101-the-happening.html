Part III


I guess that by now you must be asking yourself if this thing will ever come to a proper ending. And you are absolutely right. This collection of advices on how to please our algorithmic overlords and achieve artistic sublimation and financial plenitude and sexual satisfaction and grammatical correctness and social acceptance and a suitable fashion sense and all those other things you have been dreaming about, indeed, all those things you have been demanding from your website and social media accounts even at the risk of alienating your friends and family, while writing keyword laden blogs posts about nothing whatsoever that frequently spiral out of control in ever more unhinged, weirdly (un)personal (dis)pleasing nonsense that—where was I going with all this? Ah, yes, something about art marketing and SEO.

Did I ever tell you about my friend Person? Yes, that is his real name. His last name, actually. He never told me his first name. Not even while drunk or high on synthetic mushrooms.

He used to live on the lower floors of the Vermilion Towers, back when the place was still livable.

Anyway, my friend Person got quite famous for a short period of time. But that was later. Let me start at the beginning, which was before. Sort of.

Person was a writer. That is, he wrote. Not just letters, words, phrases, paragraphs, but real things. Mind objects. The sort of thing that gets into your brain and stays there, festering, growing, evolving, creating new DNAs inside your DNA.

"You see," he once said to me over a glass of blue, synthetic moonshine, the kind you don't find anymore, not in the towers, anyway. "The thing is..." I could almost feel his mind working. "You have to do it then, you see?" I nodded and sipped a few more drops of moonshine.

...a paradox of astral incompetence...

"Yes," I said. "I can see it starting to form."

...by a mechanism of disasters...

Just like that, a kind of poem began to evolve inside my mind. Words flickered and convulsed, melted, re-formed, collapsed in clouds of chromatic dust.

By then, Person was barely a person anymore (ha-ha!). More like a loose collection of thoughts, intentions, half-phrases, hidden gestures. He was there, but then he wasn't. You'd heard him ask a question, but by the time you open your mouth to answer there was no one there.

Then you'd find him sleeping in your couch, next to a pipe and an empty bottle of absinthe.

This kind of non way of non being, everywhere and nowhere at the same time, drove the algorithms crazy. The little bastards just couldn't figure him out.


I only know this because the agents came knocking on my door one evening, asking for Person.

They accused him of being legacy, non-conformant with the new, proper ways of being. He was immune to engagement, but also a source of engagement, just not the right type of engagement.

"The engagement, you see." The agent walked back and forth and around my couch. "We cannot allow this to go on."

I shrugged and told him I didn't understand.

"Everything's meta-reversed, you see," he insisted.

As usual with agents, he was wearing a black turtleneck and smelled like he hadn't showered in a long, long time.

"I have nothing to share," I replied.

Later that day, while I sat in my kitchen eating an orchid sandwich, Person suddenly appeared and immediately began to talk about having been passed out on cyber-morphine and something about translucent rhythms on a starry night.

"The heat was here," I said.

"I can smell their absence," he said, frowning.

"They said you are an enemy of the algorithms."


A few days later, he went viral. Someone posted an A.I.-modified piece of cyber-art containing excerpts from Person's mind-objects, and by lunchtime he was already the most sought-after celebrity of that day.

By tea-time, all hell broke loose.

It was later reported on some underground newsletters that the algorithms had gotten stuck in a never-ending loop of recursively, inwardly searching hyper-surveillance parallelism (what this really means, I don't know...).

By evening, the Commissary of Network Defense posted a mind-object video where he personally recited from the Texts of Foreshadowing:

"On his mugisstosst surface. With a bob, bob, bottledby. Blob. When the waves give up yours the soil may for me. Sometime then, somewhere there, I wrote me hopes and buried the page when I heard Thy voice, ruddery dunner, so loud that none but, and left it to lie till a kissmess coming. So content me now. Lss."

That was the last time anyone saw the Commissary.

As for Person, he sent me a letter a few weeks later, complaining about feeling incapable of being nowhere.

Now that you know everything there is to know about SEO, art marketing, fame, fortune, sex, money, and our overlords, the algorithms, don't forget to spread your new-found knowledge!
