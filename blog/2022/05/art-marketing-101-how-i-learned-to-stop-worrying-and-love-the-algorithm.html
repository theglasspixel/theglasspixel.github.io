Art Marketing 101: How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love the Algorithm

In the beginning was the algorithm, and the algorithm was good.

The algorithm cast his all-seeing eye upon the interwebs and saw all the keywords, and all the blog posts, and the videos, and memes. And being not very smart, --or not smart at all, really-- the algorithm couldn't tell the good stuff from the bad stuff.

But the algorithm saw that some stuff was shinier, noisier, more insisting, more engaging, and it said This is the Stuff.

Since then, legions of magicians, wizards and witches, priests and monks, cave-dwellers and astronauts, geeks and nerds, those who know how-to, and those who do not, have been hard at work trying to divine the hidden knowledge of the Mighty Algorithm.

Some say you have to think like an algorithm. Others that you have to be an algorithm. Others still will say that you will never be good enough, that only after your death will the algorithm notice you.

Fear not, dear reader and fellow artist. What follows is a detailed guide on pleasing and captivating our algorithmic overlord. No more will your data linger in the un-indexed limbo. Fame, fortune, and multitudes of followers await!


Start Art Marketing by Making an Amazing Online Portfolio

Back in the good old days, things were so much simpler: go out to the streets where the crowds roam, and start knocking passers-by in the head with your freshly painted masterpieces.

It's true that some of your visual marvels would end up destroyed by enraged philistines, but it was still worth it: once or twice a week you could even afford a meal down in the lower sublevels.

But that was then, which was different from now.

Anyway, what you need to do right now is an online art portfolio, as it really is the best way to knock people over the head with your art nowadays.

It doesn't matter what kind of art you're making, what you are trying to say or achieve, if you're joyous or married, hideous or handsome, earthling or one of those other fellows, an online art portfolio is definitely what you need.


Use a Website Builder

A website builder is a kind of wasp that lives in the denser parts of the forest. To catch one, you have to pay for a radio transmitter capable of broadcasting your brain waves out onto the ether.

Preferably, you should drink a copious amount of absinthe before starting transmission.

If all goes well, one of these wonderfully terrifying wasps will nest under your pillow during the night. Just be careful not to freak out when you finally wake up with a massive hangover.

Open your windows and let the pollen in.

Finally, sit down with your new wasp-friend in front of your personal computer and start work.

Choose a layout that is both fearsome and bug-free, while keeping in mind that visitors should not collapse into psychic horror or hallucinogenic absorption. In those states, they would become unable to purchase your products and you would starve.

In general, you want something that reflects not you as you are, but you as the markets want to see you.

The wasp will live with you for a period no longer than one week. After that, you are left to your own devices, so be sure to make the most out of your temporary friend.


Curate that Artful Content

Gingerly pick up all your beloved artworks and drop'hem off a tall window. Do this at a time when authorities are not around, or you might get into trouble. Some of your heavier masterpieces are sure to cause grave injuries on the crowd below.

But worry not.

From the relative security of your window, observe the sudden chaos inflicted on your fellow citizens, and calmly wait for the proverbial dust to settle.

What you'll see next is the miracle of curation: notice how some--very few--of your artworks have survived the savagery of the angered crowd.

Okay, I'll be more honest than I probably should: your actual best works were stolen by the more savvy members of said crowd, and in the coming weeks and months you'll probably see them being sold for unbelievable prices in multiple markets around the internet.

Such is modern life. Worry not, forget about it, and move on.

Anyway, all the methods I'm describing here have been thoroughly tested, analyzed, re-analyzed, poked, prodded, dissected, thrown off tall windows, fed to the Almighty Algorithm, and so on---you know how these things work.

The good news is that you just got rid of a good percentage of your masterpieces, which means that your potential clients are now much less likely to become overwhelmed by your portfolio.

You should repeat this entire ordeal once or twice a year to keep things fresh.

Also, don't forget to water your vine plants. They do keep your apartment from falling apart!


Your Artist Statement and About Me Page

"I am the artist", says the artist to the imaginary audience living in his mind.

If the audience starts to boo!, wait a day or two and start over. Wait a fortnight if needed be.

"But who is this person?" your audience will say. To which you will reply "I am the artist!"

After they stop laughing, you can press on by saying things like "I always had a passion for gardenias and dragonflies," or "I have a deep appreciation for the beauty in nature," or "I dream of fungi diffusion," or "my favorite color is cerulean, but I find heliotrope to be much too odorous."

Remember: you are always your best judge. Believing your own lies is an essential first step for having everybody else believe those same lies.


Start Blogging

Words. The algorithm likes words. The algorithm loves words.

The only thing the algorithm loves more than words is the bilateral symmetry of an earwig while it falls from deep space. But we will leave that subject for another text.

Fade out to an old 1930s movie. The writer sits down in front of his trusty typing machine. Clack clack clack. He is writing the first blog post in the history of mankind.

This is a smart writer, you see. He's getting all of his stuff done in advance.

"Words, words, words," we hear him say. "You've never seen so many goddamned words."

If there's one thing writers love to do, is complain. And when you start doing your own writing, you will also complain a lot.

Anyway, as our writer finishes one page, he nervously glances at his wristwatch: "five minutes in and only one page done? Goddammit! I think I'm done for the day."

He gulps a glass of whiskey and throws the page into an old smelly shoebox.

"One blog post ready to go!"

What more do you want for inspiration? Start writing now! Fame and fortune are sure to follow any day soon.


Sell Your Art Online

The trick is to always fall asleep in a field of artemisia flowers, letting your subconscious sink in a vast immersion of subliminal artistic creations.

Yes, that is all there is to it.

Well, I mean, this is only the first in a series of blogs posts where I let my secrets fly out to the open, so there is surely much more to come, but for now just sit with this information and let it sink deep into your mind. Sooner than you think, the universe will start to phase itself in accordance to your own brain waves and all will be well.
