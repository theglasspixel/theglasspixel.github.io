Main Street

Main Street. It rained all morning. The cracked pavement is like a swamp covered in green leafs and big fat bugs.

This piece of the suburbs looks weirdly "undeveloped". I mean, you can still see the houses and lamp-posts. There's a kindergarten with swings and slides. Plus parked cars, only half corroded by rust.

Around noon, I sat down in what once was a restaurant. It had a wonderful stink of old grease and cigarette ashes. I found a can of beans in the kitchen and ate the whole thing just like that. I kind of doubt my stomach is going to take it without making a big fuss over it, but for now I'm good.

Then I unpacked my drawing material and did several sketches of the tables, chairs, espresso machine, and so on. I'm terrible at realistic drawing, so I think I'll burn all that stuff next time I light a fire. I should stick with abstract painting and nothing else.

Again I heard some dog barking not so far away. I should be careful, lest the dog comes with a human. Or several. That would not be good.
