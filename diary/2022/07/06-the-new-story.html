The New Story

A rowboat parked down the street, leaning against a two-story house now completely covered with vine plants and sinking slowly into the swampy terrain. I saw a praying-mantis there the size of my arm. Terrifying thing.

The streets here are like lakes. The water is green and dead calm.

I'm writing a new short story about a fellow still living here. He's a doctor and he works at the nearby hospital. He's a OB-GYN and has seen all kinds of horrible stuff during the Recovery. Now he just drags his depressed self from home to work and back again. The only person he usually sees is an old nurse. They hate each other even more than they hate themselves. Once in a while someone stumbles out of the jungle and into the hospital, only to die right there of some horrible infection or parasitic infestation.

"I don't think we are very good at what we do," the nurse likes to say.

There's a room in the hospital where they never enter. A pregnant woman died there during delivery. The kid was stillborn. As the procedure happened during the night, both the main character and the nurse left the two corpses resting there until morning... Only to came back next day to find an entire ecosystem already fully developed. The insect noises were almost deafening. Mold had grown to cover the entire corridor. Everything had that disgustingly sweet smell mixed with flesh decay.

"We should go live in the towers," the medic says.

"I'd rather have sex with a spider," the quips back responds.

Anyway, I'm sure it's going to be a wonderful story. Don't you think?
