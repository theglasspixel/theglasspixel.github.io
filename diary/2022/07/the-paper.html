The Paper

I found a damaged piece of paper from an old book:

I have divided the heavens,
I have cleft the horizon,
I have traversed the earth,
following upon his footsteps.
The Mighty KHU taketh away,
because, behold,
I am provided with his magical words
for millions of years.
I eat with my mount,
I crush my food with my jawbones.
Behold, I am the god who is the lord of the uat;
may there be given unto me, Osiris Ani,
these things in perpetuity
without fail or lessening.


This thing was inside an old, empty bird nest, with a dry, but otherwise intact egg laying on top.

Too bad there aren't any birds in this part of the world anymore.

I tried to draw something that might represent this text, but I didn't get too far. I got to think that maybe I should make something more real. Like carving up a tree, make a sculpture out of old branches and insect corpses. Something that smells of old earth and mold and pollen.

Earlier this morning, I saw a couple of drones flying by. Luckily, I was still inside the abandoned shed where I spent the night. I'm getting used to sleeping on the floor. It's uncomfortable, but at least I don't have the feeling of sleeping with ghosts.
