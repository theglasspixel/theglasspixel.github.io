The Museum

Yesterday, I found an old museum. Some large windows are almost intact, so I managed to notice the sun reflecting through the thick foliage.

The main hall was crawling with spiders. Big and hairy, at least fist-sized, and with long legs. They seem peaceful, though. Or maybe just lazy. I have no idea how they can feed themselves. Maybe they eat those enormous butterflies I've seen flying around? Those things are scary... Also, one of the spiders was kind of singing, somewhat like a bird, with strange, long, sonorous notes. That was sort of pleasant, actually.

I thought it would be nice to sketch a couple of those spiders, so I sat down near the entrance and scribbled a little bit with the charcoal pieces I found the day before.

After a while, the lines kind of started to dance to the sound of that lazy spider-song.

About an hour later, I woke up feeling rested and with a slight headache. A new spider had started a huge web right next to me. It smelled sweet and warm, like the breath of someone who's just eaten a caramel.

Anyway, I wonder if the authorities in the towers have already noticed my absence. If they try to track me down, I'm going to be in deep trouble.
