A Stroll Through the Suburbs

Yesterday, I decided to go for a walk in the suburbs.

There are some folks still living there. Old people mostly. I really don’t know how they manage to survive. Are they all immune? It’s strange, to say the least. Then again, there isn’t really a shortage of food or water. Quite the contrary, really.

I first took an inter-tower train all the way to the Byzantium Towers. The guards there were very suspicious, asking a lot of questions, making me wait, the usual tactics…

I had planned to stay out for a week or two, but the pigs apprehended my camera and drawing material on the excuse of ‘danger of creating non-networked works of art,’ whatever that means, so I’m dictating this using my encrypted wrist connection. Hopefully it won’t get absorbed before I get back home.

If you keep on walking, the towers will eventually start to disappear on the horizon. It’s a strange image. Frightening too.

I should probably dictate a much more detailed account of this little trip, but I’m too tired right now. Hopefully tomorrow I will feel more lively…
